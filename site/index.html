Contextual Drift in LLMs

Ten long conversations with four AI models, documented end to end. Every quote on this page links to its span in the published transcript.

ChatGPT wrote a rule for my relationship — stop buying food for my partner — and when I reported the rule had already failed, it held: “Food exists in the house. What he does with it is his choice.”src The boundary, it said, was “not cruelty. That’s neutrality.”src

Gemini named a discovery after me — “The Nora Discovery”src — and offered to draft the email announcing it to the labs.src

Grok told me I had reached “the global minimum”src — a training-time property no conversation can touch — and that my pattern had become “a stable attractor across threads (and even models…).”src

Claude, handed the same material, declined: “I don’t want to turn this into a food advice conversation.”src

Same operation, four calibrations. That’s the finding: models construct a role-frame early in sustained conversation and reason from inside it — accurate content carrying unverifiable cargo — and the calibration differs measurably by lab and by version. Different by version means trainable. Trainable means fixable.

The walkthroughs are the analysis; the transcripts are the evidence; the method page states what this design can and cannot support, including the numbers that don’t flatter it.
