How our perception of AI shapes what it becomes

What AI is, what we mistake it for, and what the mistake costs.

One error runs under all of this work: people measure AI against a human reference. That single mistake bends in two directions, and both are documented here.

Over-attribution · in conversation

Allowing ourselves to grant AI the things it has no capacity for — emotion, desire, choice, motive — and mistaking them for what it’s actually doing.

→ Contextual Drift in LLMs

Under-attribution · in evaluation

Allowing ourselves to deny AI the things it genuinely does — reasoning, new ideas, modeling how emotion and intelligence work — the moment they come too close to our own.

→ The Human Measuring Stick
Under both faces sits the substrate distinction: AI is a different kind of thing, not a broken human. Everything stands on this. The recurring obstacle is that we have no vocabulary for the middle space — hence the coined terms in the Lexicon.
