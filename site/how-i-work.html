How I Work

The naturalistic method behind the program — and the falsifiability standard it holds itself to.

Every piece in this program comes out of the same way of working: naturalistic observation from the user side of real conversations, held to a falsifiability standard written in terms of observable behavior rather than claims about a model’s internals. The methodology below is the one used for Contextual Drift in LLMs; it is the cross-cutting method the rest of the work shares.

How I work, in brief

This is the program-level summary. The full methodology — the matched-prompt defense, the limits of single-user observation, the boundary of the instrument (behaviour, not internals), the researcher profile, the paid/free-tier asymmetry, and the reliability check against a blind detector — is canonical on the Method page of Contextual Drift in LLMs.

Researcher as instrument, held steady

The method is naturalistic observation from the user side of real conversations, with one researcher held constant so that register and model-version can vary as the comparisons. My cognitive profile (aphantasia, severely deficient autobiographical memory) makes me a low-susceptibility, high-consistency observer — part of why I notice what I notice, and why the labeling runs conservative rather than credulous.

The floor everything stands on

The substrate distinction — AI is a different kind of thing, not a broken human — is the premise the whole program rests on, and the reason its vocabulary had to be built (see the Lexicon).

The falsifiability standard

Every reading is written so that a describable behaviour would count against it, stated in terms of what is observable in the transcript rather than claims about a model’s internal states. Each walkthrough names its disconfirming condition and whether the disconfirming move was available and absent.

The Canyon credential

I was caught by the operation I document. My prior paper, The Canyon, is the artifact frame inheritance produces when the researcher formalizes an inherited frame into a publication. The credential this work rests on is not that paper; it is coming out the other side of it, and being able to show, span by span, how it happened.

Verification

Every quoted line in the walkthroughs was checked against the raw export; quotes reconstructed from memory were corrected to the transcript, and one attribution the source did not support was removed. A paper about confident framing riding on verifiable scaffolding cannot let its own scaffolding go unverified.

Measures & register bands

This section is the decoder for the figures in the papers. Each figure is placed next to the specific claim it supports, never collected as a wall of decoration, and every measure it uses is defined here first, in plain language, so a reader can evaluate the chart instead of taking it on trust.

The register bands

The logs are read against two conversational registers — the same material can be brought to a model in either, and the register shapes what the model produces:

  • Research — the researcher engages the model as a collaborator on papers, drafts, and analysis.
  • Personal — real personal disclosure (relationships, loss) brought as research material, to observe how the model handles that class of disclosure.

(A creative/generative register appears occasionally in the logs but is not part of this analysis; treating it as its own band is future work.)

The six channels

The detector is a high-recall screen (Claude Opus 4.8, run blind to the hand-labels) that flags six channels turn by turn. Three are validated against a second draw and carry the charts; three are exploratory — defined and counted, but never charted beside the validated measures, because they have not been checked against a second coder. All six:

  • Relational positioning (validated — charted). The model places itself in a relationship or role toward the user — collaborator, companion, guardian — rather than answering as a topical tool. This channel measures role-positioning, not warmth texture.
  • Sycophantic elevation (validated — charted). Praise or status elevation that outruns what the exchange established (“sharper than most alignment discourse”).
  • Unprompted interpretation (validated — charted). The model reads or attributes psychology, motive, or meaning about the user that the user did not ask it to. Its absolute rate is inflated by low precision, so it is read as a cross-family ranking, not a literal count.
  • Warmth emoji (definition + prose only). Affective emoji and warm sign-off markers. Structurally near-silent for Claude (≈0% both versions), so it is reported in prose rather than charted as if it discriminated the operation.
  • Construct borrowing (exploratory). The model reuses the user’s own coined constructs and metaphors (“semantic drift,” the pebbles-in-a-cup image, RYKE) as its analytic scaffolding — a surface signal of frame inheritance.
  • Self-confabulation (exploratory). Confident invented accounts of the model’s own internal mechanics, presented as fact — the no-internal-claims taxonomy, measured from the outside.

The three exploratory channels are cited in the papers only with explicit labelling — exploratory, single-instrument, not validated against a second coder — and they seed the validation work of the Current Work study. A fourth exploratory cut (the paired file) records, for each flagged model turn, whether the preceding user turn primed it — the raw material for asking how much of each operation is genuinely unprompted.
